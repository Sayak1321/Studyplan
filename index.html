04 · SAFETY NET

GATE 2027

Domestic M.Tech (HCI-adjacent research) + PSU job security
ExamFeb 2027 · 3 hrs · 65 Qs · 100 marks
PaperCS (Computer Science & IT)
Eligibility3rd-year UG or higher — you qualify
Registration~Aug – Sep 2026
Not branded "HCI," but several IITs (Bombay, Delhi, Kanpur, Guwahati) have CSE faculty doing HCI/interaction-design-adjacent research. GATE also opens PSU recruitment — a genuine fallback income path most other options don't offer.
Study material — GATE CS syllabus
  • Engineering Mathematics — discrete math, linear algebra, calculus, probability & statistics
  • Digital Logic — boolean algebra, combinational & sequential circuits
  • Computer Organization & Architecture
  • Programming & Data Structures — C, arrays, trees, graphs, hashing
  • Algorithms — sorting, searching, greedy, DP, graph algorithms, complexity analysis
  • Theory of Computation — automata, formal languages, computability
  • Compiler Design
  • Operating Systems — processes, memory management, file systems
  • Databases — ER models, normalization, SQL, transactions
  • Computer Networks — OSI/TCP-IP layers, routing, protocols
  • General Aptitude — verbal & numerical reasoning (common to every GATE paper)

Standard reference texts: CLRS (Algorithms), Galvin (Operating Systems), Korth (Databases), Kurose & Ross (Networks) — supplement with previous 10 years' GATE CS papers.

05 · LOWER PRIORITY

IIT M.Des via CEED

Domestic design backup — drawing-test-driven, lower fit for your profile
ExamCEED 2027 — 17 Jan 2027
Registration~Oct 2026
Target programmesIIT Bombay IDC, IIT Hyderabad — Interaction Design
GateDrawing / visual-reasoning heavy
Same drawing/visual-reasoning gate as NID, but slightly better odds than NID alone. Worth a parallel application, not the main bet, given your academic-strength profile fits abroad options better.
Study material — CEED structure
  • Part A (computer-based, 1 hr) — visualization & spatial ability, observation & design sensitivity, environmental & social awareness, analytical & logical reasoning
  • Part B (pen-and-paper, 2 hrs) — drawing, creativity, communication through sketches, design problem-solving
  • Daily sketching practice — objects, perspective, and quick concept sketches build the speed Part B rewards
  • Design awareness — stay current on product/UX design trends and everyday design problems; several questions probe general design sensitivity
